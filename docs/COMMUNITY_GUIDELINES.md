# Community Guidelines

## Welcome to the TiXL Community

The TiXL community is a diverse group of creators, developers, artists, and enthusiasts who share a passion for real-time motion graphics and open-source collaboration. These guidelines help ensure our community remains welcoming, productive, and inspiring for everyone.

## Our Community Values

### Creativity and Innovation
We celebrate creative expression and encourage experimentation with TiXL's powerful real-time graphics capabilities.

### Collaboration and Learning
We believe in the power of shared knowledge and mutual support. Whether you're a beginner or expert, there's always something to learn and teach.

### Quality and Craftsmanship
We strive for excellence in our work and encourage high standards in code, documentation, and creative output.

### Inclusivity and Respect
We welcome people of all backgrounds, skill levels, and perspectives. Every member deserves respect and consideration.

### Open Source Spirit
We embody the open-source ethos of transparency, collaboration, and community-driven development.

## Communication Guidelines

### Respectful Discourse

**Do:**
- Use welcoming and inclusive language
- Focus on ideas and contributions, not personal attributes
- Ask questions when you don't understand
- Offer constructive feedback and suggestions
- Acknowledge others' contributions and expertise

**Don't:**
- Use discriminatory, harassing, or offensive language
- Make personal attacks or engage in character assassination
- Dominate conversations or dismiss others' perspectives
- Share inappropriate content or NSFW material
- Spam or flood channels with off-topic content

### Inclusive Communication

**Language:**
- Use gender-neutral language when possible
- Avoid idioms or cultural references that may not be universally understood
- Be mindful of different time zones and cultural contexts
- Use clear, simple language for technical discussions

**Accessibility:**
- Provide alt text for images and screenshots
- Use proper formatting for code and technical content
- Consider different communication preferences
- Be patient with non-native speakers

### Technical Discussions

**When asking for help:**
1. Search existing documentation and issues first
2. Provide clear context and goals
3. Include system information and versions
4. Share relevant code or project files
5. Describe what you've already tried

**When providing help:**
1. Be patient and encouraging
2. Ask clarifying questions if needed
3. Provide step-by-step guidance
4. Share relevant documentation links
5. Follow up to ensure the issue is resolved

## Content Guidelines

### Creative Content

**Sharing Projects:**
- Include project files when possible
- Describe your creative process
- Credit inspirations and references
- Tag appropriately for discoverability

**Tutorials and Guides:**
- Ensure accuracy and clarity
- Test with current TiXL versions
- Include screenshots or videos
- Welcome feedback and improvements

**Showcase Etiquette:**
- Give credit to collaborators
- Respect intellectual property
- Avoid inappropriate content
- Engage positively with comments and feedback

### Technical Content

**Code Sharing:**
- Follow project coding standards
- Include adequate documentation
- Test thoroughly before sharing
- Be open to feedback and improvements

**Documentation:**
- Keep information current and accurate
- Use clear, concise language
- Include examples and use cases
- Link to relevant resources

## Conflict Resolution

### Minor Disagreements

**Best Practices:**
- Take a break if emotions are high
- Focus on the issue, not the person
- Seek to understand different perspectives
- Find common ground and compromise
- Ask for mediator help if needed

### Serious Issues

**Code of Conduct Violations:**
1. Document the incident with details
2. Contact moderators or project maintainers
3. Allow proper investigation process
4. Respect decisions and outcomes
5. Focus on community healing and improvement

**Technical Disputes:**
1. Use objective criteria and evidence
2. Consider expert opinions and community input
3. Pilot different approaches if possible
4. Make decisions based on community benefit
5. Document decisions and rationale

## Getting Started as a New Member

### Introduction Process

1. **Join Discord and introduce yourself**
   - Share your background and interests
   - What drew you to TiXL?
   - What would you like to learn or contribute?

2. **Explore the documentation**
   - Read the quick start guide
   - Watch tutorial videos
   - Try building your first project

3. **Start small**
   - Ask questions in help channels
   - Explore existing projects and examples
   - Share your first creation in #show-and-tell

### Finding Your Place

**For Developers:**
- Join #development and #operators-discussion
- Review contribution guidelines
- Look for "good first issue" labels
- Join working group meetings

**For Artists:**
- Share work in #show-and-tell and #art-and-creative
- Participate in creative challenges
- Collaborate on community projects
- Provide user perspective feedback

**For Educators:**
- Contribute to documentation
- Share teaching materials and examples
- Organize or host community events
- Mentor new community members

## General Licensing Questions

### Q1.1: What license does TiXL use?
**A:** TiXL uses an enhanced MIT License with additional clauses addressing modern software development concerns. The license includes:
- Standard MIT permissions (use, copy, modify, distribute)
- Patent license grants for contributor patents
- Trademark usage guidelines
- Export control compliance requirements
- Clear attribution requirements

### Q1.2: Is TiXL open source?
**A:** Yes, TiXL is open source software. It can be:
- Used freely for any purpose
- Modified and distributed
- Integrated into proprietary software
- Used commercially without fees

The enhanced MIT license ensures both freedom and legal clarity.

### Q1.3: Can I use TiXL in my project?
**A:** Yes, you can use TiXL in any project by:
1. Including the TiXL license text in your project
2. Maintaining attribution to TiXL Contributors
3. Including copyright notices
4. Complying with export control laws if applicable

No additional agreements are needed for most uses.

### Q1.4: What are the basic requirements for using TiXL?
**A:** The basic requirements are:
- Include the MIT license text
- Include copyright notice: "Copyright (c) 2025 TiXL Contributors"
- Use software "as is" without warranty
- Comply with trademark guidelines if using TiXL branding

### Q1.5: Can I modify TiXL?
**A:** Yes, you can modify TiXL. Modified versions must:
- Indicate that modifications were made
- Maintain all original copyright notices
- Include your own copyright for modifications
- Comply with the same license terms

### Q1.6: What happens if I violate the license?
**A:** License violations may result in:
- Request to comply with license terms
- Legal action for copyright infringement
- Termination of rights to use TiXL
- Potential damages and legal fees

Minor violations typically result in educational outreach.

### Q1.7: How do I report a license violation?
**A:** Report license violations to: **legal@tixl-project.org**
Include:
- Description of violation
- Evidence of violation
- Your contact information
- Preferred resolution approach

### Q1.8: Is TiXL compatible with other open source licenses?
**A:** TiXL's MIT license is compatible with most open source licenses:
- ✅ MIT, Apache 2.0, BSD, ISC, Unlicense
- ⚠️ GPL, LGPL (copyleft considerations)
- ❌ No compatibility issues known with major licenses

**A:** Yes, TiXL can be used commercially under the MIT license:

### Q2.3: What commercial activities are allowed?
**A:** You may:

- Offer services using TiXL

- Integrate TiXL into existing products

## Contributor Licensing Questions

### Q5.1: Do I need to sign a contributor agreement?
**A:** For most contributions, no separate agreement is needed. However:
- Large or significant contributions may require ICLA
- Corporate contributions require CCLA
- Patent-related contributions need explicit licenses
- Strategic partnerships may require additional agreements

### Q5.2: What rights do I retain as a contributor?
**A:** As a contributor, you retain:
- Copyright to your original contributions
- Patent rights to your innovations
- Recognition and attribution
- Community participation rights
- Continued use rights under the license

### Q5.3: Can I contribute anonymously?
**A:** While anonymous contributions are technically possible, they are discouraged because:
- Attribution is important for recognition
- Legal agreements require identity
- Community trust depends on transparency
- Potential licensing disputes require known parties

### Q5.4: What happens if my employer objects to my contribution?
**A:** If your employer objects:
- You must stop contributing immediately
- Previous contributions may need review
- Employer rights take precedence
- Consult with employment counsel
- Consider using personal time/equipment

### Q5.5: Can I contribute code I wrote at work?
**A:** This depends on your employment situation:
- **Generally no:** If it was created as work product
- **Case-by-case:** If created outside work duties
- **Usually yes:** If explicitly assigned to you
- **Consult counsel:** When in doubt, get legal advice

### Q5.6: How do I handle third-party code in my contributions?
**A:** When contributing code:
- Ensure all third-party code is properly licensed
- Include appropriate license notices
- Avoid copying proprietary or trade secret code
- Document third-party dependencies
- Get permission when uncertain

### Q5.7: What if I want to withdraw my contribution?
**A:** Once contributed, code generally cannot be withdrawn because:
- License grants are typically irrevocable
- Project and users rely on contributions
- Legal framework expects permanence
- Consider community impact before contributing

### Q5.8: Can I contribute to competing projects?
**A:** Generally yes, but be careful about:
- Not sharing TiXL trade secrets
- Respecting confidentiality obligations
- Avoiding direct code copying
- Maintaining professional ethics
- Checking employment agreements

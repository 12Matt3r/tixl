# Enforcement Procedures

## Overview

These enforcement procedures outline how the TiXL community handles violations of our Code of Conduct, community guidelines, and governance policies. Our goal is to maintain a safe, welcoming, and productive environment for all community members while ensuring fairness and transparency in all enforcement actions.

## Types of Violations

### Code of Conduct Violations

**Harassment:**
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- Personal attacks or threats
- stalking or following

**Discrimination:**
- Offensive verbal comments related to personal characteristics
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Photographing or recording someone without consent

**Unprofessional Behavior:**
- Retaliation against individuals who report violations
- Advocating for harassment or discrimination
- Encouraging harmful or illegal behavior
- Distribution of inappropriate material

### Community Guidelines Violations

**Communication:**
- Spamming or flooding channels
- Off-topic discussions in dedicated channels
- Use of discriminatory language
- Sharing inappropriate content

**Technical:**
- Malicious code or spam contributions
- Abuse of community resources
- Attempting to circumvent security measures
- Disruption of development processes

### Governance Violations

**Process Abuse:**
- Circumventing established procedures
- Misrepresentation of roles or authority
- Abuse of merge or release privileges
- Manipulation of community processes

**Conflict of Interest:**
- Self-dealing in official decisions
- Hidden conflicts of interest
- Using position for personal gain
- Bias in code review or decision-making

## Reporting Process

### Initial Report

**How to Report:**
1. **Document the Incident**: Record date, time, participants, and detailed description
2. **Gather Evidence**: Save screenshots, messages, or other relevant materials
3. **Contact Moderators**: Email moderation@tixl-project.org or contact a core maintainer
4. **Maintain Confidentiality**: Avoid public discussion until resolution

**Information to Include:**
- Description of the violation
- When and where it occurred
- Who was involved
- Witnesses or other evidence
- Any previous similar incidents
- What resolution you're seeking

### Anonymous Reporting

We accept anonymous reports but encourage providing contact information for follow-up questions. Anonymous reporters can use a secure email service or contact through GitHub's private issue system.

### Third-Party Reporting

If you witness a violation involving someone who may not report it themselves, please report it. We treat all reports seriously, regardless of who submits them.

## Investigation Process

### Initial Assessment (24-48 hours)

**Step 1: Acknowledge Receipt**
- Confirm receipt of report to reporter
- Assign case number for tracking
- Provide timeline for initial assessment

**Step 2: Initial Review**
- Determine severity and urgency
- Assess immediate safety concerns
- Consider interim protective measures

**Step 3: Investigator Assignment**
- Assign appropriate investigator(s)
- Consider conflicts of interest
- Ensure diverse perspectives in investigation

### Investigation (1-2 weeks)

**Evidence Gathering:**
- Review documentation and evidence
- Interview involved parties and witnesses
- Consult with subject matter experts if needed
- Maintain confidentiality throughout process

**Investigation Standards:**
- Preponderance of evidence standard
- Fair and impartial process
- No predetermined conclusions
- Transparent documentation of findings

**Investigation Timeline:**
- Week 1: Evidence gathering and interviews
- Week 2: Analysis and preliminary findings
- Extensions possible for complex cases

### Decision Making

**Decision Committee:**
- Two core maintainers and one community member
- Recuse themselves if conflicts of interest
- Review investigation findings together

**Decision Criteria:**
- Severity and impact of violation
- Intent vs. negligence
- Previous history and warnings
- Community context and impact
- Likelihood of recurrence

## Enforcement Actions

### Warning (First-time minor violations)

**Procedure:**
- Written warning issued
- Explanation of acceptable behavior
- Timeline for behavioral changes
- Warning recorded in confidential file

**Appeal:**
- 48 hours to request review
- Review by different maintainer
- Decision communicated in writing

### Required Apology (Inappropriate behavior)

**Procedure:**
- Public or private apology required
- Acknowledgment of impact on community
- Commitment to changed behavior
- Community notification if public apology

**Monitoring:**
- 30-day behavioral monitoring period
- Regular check-ins with community moderators
- Community feedback collection

### Temporary Suspension (Serious or repeated violations)

**Duration:**
- 1 week to 3 months depending on severity
- May include removal from community spaces
- Access to public repositories may be restricted

**Process:**
- Written notification of suspension terms
- Clear timeline and conditions for return
- Regular review during suspension period
- Return requires behavioral commitment

**Appeal:**
- Written appeal required after 1 week of suspension
- Review by full moderation team
- Community input considered in decision

### Permanent Ban (Severe or repeated serious violations)

**Criteria:**
- Severe violations with significant community impact
- Pattern of repeated violations after multiple warnings
- Behavior that poses ongoing risk to community safety
- Violations of legal boundaries

**Process:**
- Full moderation team decision required
- Written explanation of reasons
- Opportunity for final written statement
- Community notification if appropriate

**Appeal:**
- One-time appeal after 6 months
- Requires substantial evidence of changed behavior
- Community support demonstration required
- Full moderation team re-evaluation

## Special Considerations

### Public Figure Exception

For community leaders, maintainers, or public representatives:
- Higher standards of behavior expected
- Faster escalation for violations
- Increased accountability to community
- Proactive steps to model good behavior required

### Cultural Sensitivity

When evaluating behavior:
- Consider cultural context and backgrounds
- Provide education and coaching for cultural misunderstandings
- Distinguish between ignorance and malice
- Allow for learning and growth opportunities

### Mental Health Considerations

Recognizing that online behavior can be affected by:
- Mental health challenges
- Personal crises or stress
- Medication side effects
- Neurodiversity or communication differences

**Response:**
- Provide resources and support when appropriate
- Consider accommodations for neurodiversity
- Encourage professional help when needed
- Balance accountability with compassion

## Appeals Process

### Appeal Eligibility

Appeals are available for:
- Factual errors in investigation
- Procedural violations in enforcement process
- Disproportionate enforcement action
- New evidence or changed circumstances

Appeals are not available for:
- Acknowledged violations
- Standard enforcement actions
- Cases where appeal was already processed

### Appeal Process

**Step 1: Written Appeal**
- Must be submitted within 30 days
- Clearly state grounds for appeal
- Include supporting evidence or documentation
- Written in professional, respectful tone

**Step 2: Appeal Review**
- Different moderator reviews the appeal
- Investigation file re-examined
- Community input solicited if appropriate
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks for decision

**Step 3: Appeal Decision**
- Written decision with rationale
- May uphold, modify, or reverse original decision
- Decision communicated to all parties
- Final decision unless new evidence emerges

## Record Keeping and Transparency

### Confidentiality

**Maintained Confidential:**
- Personal information of complainants and respondents
- Details of investigations
- Specific enforcement actions taken
- Individual behavioral records

**Publicly Available:**
- Community safety announcements when necessary
- General statistics on enforcement actions
- Updated community guidelines and procedures
- Changes to enforcement policies

### Record Retention

**Investigation Records:**
- Retained for 3 years after case closure
- Secure storage with access controls
- Regular review and purging of outdated records
- Compliance with relevant privacy laws

**Statistical Reporting:**
- Annual transparency reports published
- Aggregate data on enforcement actions
- Trends in community safety metrics
- Community satisfaction with enforcement

## Prevention and Education

### Preventive Measures

**New Member Orientation:**
- Code of Conduct acknowledgment required
- Community guidelines presentation
- Values and expectations communication
- Mentor assignment for new contributors

**Regular Education:**
- Monthly community safety discussions
- Cultural competency training
- Conflict resolution skills workshops
- Mental health awareness resources

**Early Intervention:**
- Monitor for concerning patterns
- Provide coaching and support
- Address issues before they escalate
- Create pathways for success

### Community Building

**Positive Reinforcement:**
- Recognition of positive contributions
- Celebration of diverse perspectives
- Rewards for helpful community members
- Leadership development programs

**Safe Space Creation:**
- Clear channels for sensitive discussions
- Trusted moderator availability
- Anonymous reporting options
- Mental health resource sharing

## Emergency Procedures

### Immediate Threats

**Response to immediate threats:**
- Remove threat immediately
- Contact appropriate authorities if necessary
- Document incident for legal purposes
- Support affected community members
- Communicate with community about safety

**Crisis Management:**
- Activate crisis response team
- Implement emergency communication protocols
- Coordinate with platform administrators
- Ensure ongoing community safety

### Platform-Level Issues

**Discord or GitHub issues:**
- Work with platform administrators
- Implement additional monitoring
- Coordinate with other community spaces
- Maintain communication channels

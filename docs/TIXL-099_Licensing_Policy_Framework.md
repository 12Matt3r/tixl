## Overview and Philosophy

### Mission Statement

TiXL is committed to providing accessible, powerful graphics programming tools while fostering innovation, collaboration, and sustainable development. Our licensing framework balances open-source accessibility with commercial viability, ensuring long-term project sustainability.

- Enable rapid prototyping and experimentation

**2. Commercial Viability**
- Support commercial applications and enterprise adoption
- Provide flexible licensing options for businesses

**3. Legal Clarity**
- Clear, unambiguous licensing terms
- Comprehensive IP protection mechanisms
- Regulatory compliance across jurisdictions

**4. Contributor Rights**
- Fair attribution and recognition
- Retained rights for contributors
- Transparent contribution processes

## Contributor Licensing Agreements

### Individual Contributor License Agreement (ICLA)

#### Purpose and Scope
- Establish clear licensing terms for individual contributions
- Grant necessary rights for project distribution
- Provide patent protection for users
- Clarify contributor representations

#### Key Terms

**1. Copyright License Grant**
```
Contributor grants TiXL project a perpetual, irrevocable, 
non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, 
copy, modify, merge, publish, distribute, sublicense, 
and sell contributor's contributions.
```

**2. Patent License Grant**
```
Contributor grants a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free 
patent license for contributor's contributions and any 
derivatives thereof.
```

**3. Representation and Warranty**
```
Contributor represents that:
- Contributor owns or has rights to contribute the code
- Contribution is original or properly licensed
- No third-party rights are violated
- Contribution complies with project standards
```

#### Implementation Process
1. Digital signature through automated system
2. Integration with GitHub commit verification
3. Automated license header management
4. Regular compliance audits

### Corporate Contributor License Agreement (CCLA)

#### Purpose and Scope
- Enable organizational contributions
- Ensure corporate approval for contributions
- Address employment and contractor relationships
- Provide broader licensing scope

#### Corporate Requirements
- Legal entity identification
- Authorized signatory designation
- Corporate policy compliance
- Employee notification requirements

#### Key Terms

**1. Corporate Warranty**
```
Corporation warrants that:
- Corporation has right to contribute the code
- Contribution doesn't violate third-party rights
- Contributors have appropriate authorization
- Corporation will address compliance issues
```

**2. Broader License Grant**
```
Corporation grants expanded rights including:
- Commercial distribution rights
- Patent licensing for commercial use
- Trademark usage for promotion
- Modification and derivative work rights
```

### Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO)

#### Purpose
- Ensure contribution provenance
- Verify contributor authority
- Provide legal protection
- Maintain project integrity

#### DCO Text
```
Developer Certificate of Origin
Version 1.1

Copyright (C) 2004, 2006 The Linux Foundation and its contributors.

Everyone is permitted to copy and distribute verbatim copies of this
license document, but changing it is not allowed.

Developer's Certificate of Origin 1.1

By making a contribution to this project, I certify that:

(a) The contribution was created in whole or in part by me and I
    have the right to submit it under the open source license
    indicated in the file; or

(b) The contribution is based upon previous work that, to the best
    of my knowledge, is covered under an appropriate license and I
    have the right under that license to submit that work with
    modifications, describing explicitly what I changed, under
    either that license or a GNU General Public License version 2
    or later with the modifications that I contributed.

(c) I understand and agree that this project and the contribution
    are public and that a record of the contribution is maintained
    indefinitely.
```

#### Implementation
- Automated DCO checking in CI/CD
- Git commit signature verification
- Regular compliance reports
- Community education and training
